Gold

Yellow Gold

Pure gold is often too soft for practical use in jewelry, so it is alloyed with other metals to increase its strength and durability. To create yellow gold, pure gold is typically mixed with copper and sometimes silver. The proportions of these metals determine the specific shade of yellow gold, with higher copper content producing a redder hue. Our yellow gold ranges between 10k and 14k.

White Gold

White gold is created through a combination of pure gold and one or more white metals, typically nickel, palladium, or silver. The process involves alloying, which means mixing different metals together to create a new material with desirable properties.

Silver:
Silver is occasionally used in white gold alloys, especially in lower-cost jewelry. It can give the gold a slightly warmer tone compared to nickel or palladium.

Rose Gold

Rose gold, also known as pink gold or red gold, is a beautiful and popular alloy of gold. It gets its distinct pinkish or reddish hue from the combination of pure gold (which is naturally yellow) and other metals, typically copper and a small amount of silver.
